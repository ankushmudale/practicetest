7.	Explain the role of the init process in Linux.
The init process is the ancestor of all processes in a Unix-based operating system, including Linux. It is the first process started by the kernel during the system boot process and has a process ID (PID) of 1. The primary responsibility of the init process is to initialize the system and serve as the parent or grandparent of all other processes.

9.	What is the function of the df command?
The df command in Linux is used to display information about the disk space usage on a file system. It reports the amount of disk space used, available, and total space on a file system, as well as the percentage of space used.
